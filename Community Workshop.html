Spring Community Garden Workshop

Saturday, April 15th, 2025 | 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM

Learn to Grow Your Own Food

A hands-on workshop for beginners and experienced gardeners

What You'll Learn

Join us for a full day of learning about sustainable gardening practices in our local climate. This workshop is designed for community members of all skill levels who want to start growing fresh vegetables, herbs, and flowers at home. Our experienced instructors will guide you through everything from soil preparation to harvest techniques, ensuring you leave with practical knowledge you can apply immediately in your own garden or balcony containers.

Throughout the day, you'll participate in hands-on demonstrations covering essential gardening topics. We'll explore how to test and improve your soil quality, choose the right plants for each season, implement water-saving irrigation methods, and manage pests naturally without harmful chemicals. You'll also learn about companion planting techniques that help your garden thrive and maximize your growing space, whether you have a large backyard or just a small patio

Workshop Schedule

  • 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM: Soil Science & Preparation

    Understanding soil types, pH levels, composting basics, and preparing garden beds for planting

  • 10:45 AM - 12:00 PM: Seed Starting & Plant Selection

    How to choose vegetables and herbs for your region, starting seeds indoors, and transplanting seedlings

  • 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM: Lunch Break

    Enjoy a community potluck with fresh salads from local gardens (please bring a dish to share)

  • 1:00 PM - 2:15 PM: Pest Management & Garden Maintenance

    Identifying common pests, natural pest control methods, watering techniques, and seasonal maintenance

  • 2:30 PM - 3:00 PM: Q&A and Garden Planning

    Open discussion, personalized advice, and creating your own garden plan to take home

What to Bring

Please come prepared with the following items:

  1. Garden gloves and a sun hat for outdoor activities
  2. Notebook and pen for taking notes during presentations
  3. Water bottle to stay hydrated throughout the day
  4. A dish to share during the potluck lunch break
  5. Any specific questions about your garden or growing challenges
  6. Camera or phone if you want to photograph demonstration techniques

Registration Details

This workshop is completely free and open to all community members aged 16 and above. Space is limited to 40 participants to ensure everyone receives individual attention during hands-on activities. Registration is required and will be available until all spots are filled. Priority registration will be given to local residents, with remaining spots opened to neighboring communities one week before the event.

All participants will receive a complimentary packet of seasonal vegetable seeds, a soil testing kit, and a detailed planting guide for our region. You'll also gain access to our online community forum where you can continue learning, share your progress, and ask questions long after the workshop ends. Whether you're dreaming of a backyard vegetable garden or just want to grow fresh herbs on your windowsill, this workshop will give you the foundation you need to succeed.
